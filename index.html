About

Between 1901 and 2024, the Nobel Prizes and the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel were awarded 627 times to 1,012 people and organisations. The Nobel Prize is an international award administered by the Nobel Foundation in Stockholm, Sweden, and based on the fortune of Alfred Nobel, Swedish inventor and entrepreneur. In 1968, Sveriges Riksbank established the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel. Each prize consists of a medal, a personal diploma, and a cash award.

Who decides the Nobel Prizes?

Independent committees and academies select laureates based on nominations and rigorous evaluation.

Winners

What is a Nobel laureate?

A Nobel laureate is a person or organization awarded a Nobel Prize for work that “has conferred the greatest benefit to humankind.” The term laureate comes from the laurel wreath used to crown victors in ancient Greece. Since 1901, prizes have been awarded in Physics, Chemistry, Physiology or Medicine, Literature, and Peace; in 1968 the Prize in Economic Sciences was added in Alfred Nobel’s memory.

Albert Einstein

Physics — 1921 (photoelectric effect)

Explained how light can knock electrons free from metal—evidence that light acts like particles. The work powered quantum theory, even as his fame soared for relativity.

Nelson Mandela

Peace — 1993 (with F. W. de Klerk)

After 27 years in prison, he negotiated an end to apartheid and became South Africa’s first democratically elected president, centering reconciliation over revenge.

Mother Teresa

Peace — 1979

Founded the Missionaries of Charity and devoted her life to serving the poor and the dying, inspiring global volunteerism and a network of care homes.

Marie Curie

Physics — 1903 • Chemistry — 1911

First woman to win a Nobel and the only person to win in two sciences— radioactivity pioneer who also organized mobile X-ray units in WWI.

Linus Pauling

Chemistry — 1954 • Peace — 1962 (unshared)

Revealed the nature of the chemical bond, then led a global campaign against nuclear testing—one of only two people with two unshared Nobels.

John Bardeen

Physics — 1956 • 1972

Co-invented the transistor and later co-created BCS theory of superconductivity—the only two-time Physics laureate, shaping the electronics era.

Frederick Sanger

Chemistry — 1958 • 1980

Determined insulin’s structure and developed DNA sequencing methods—cornerstones of modern genomics.

ICRC

Peace — 1917 • 1944 • 1963

The International Committee of the Red Cross was recognized three times for humanitarian work during and after wars—aid under the harshest conditions.

UNHCR

Peace — 1954 • 1981

The UN refugee agency received two Peace Prizes for protecting and resettling displaced people worldwide across decades of crises.
